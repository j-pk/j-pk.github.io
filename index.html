A Reddit app that makes viewing images simple.
New

mité was designed to make the experience of looking at images, art, gifs and pictures on Reddit easier. When it comes to being the Internet’s bulletin board, Reddit can’t be beat. But as a website it can be a little cumbersome to get to those various forms of media. This is where mité steps in. It gives you what you want most first.

Leverage unique gestures to vote on your favorite images right from the main feed. Customize the menu to quickly access your favorite subreddits. Use the search feature to discover new & unique subreddits.

mité was made in Swift using Cocoa Touch frameworks, mainly UIKit, AVFoundation, and ImageIO. The app communicates heavily with Reddit's RESTFul API.

Beat Dat Budget

Beat Dat Budget was made during the joint 2-day Hackathon between SolTech & the Iron Yard. The premise of the game is to hire tech consultants and fling them, Angry Birds style, at the project hoping to reduce budget cost.

Front-end was used to make the user generated levels. The back-end processed the levels and then pushed the data to the Beat Dat Budget App were players could pick from an array of levels and play them.

UIKit, and SpriteKit was leveraged to make the game and the app communicates with a Rails backend.

QPic

QPic is a guessing game where user submit images taken from their iPhone's camera or uploaded from their computer and players guess on what the images could be.

QPic was a cross developmental project that encompassed front-end, back-end, and mobile.

The QPic app was made using UIKit, AFNetworking, AFAmazonS3Manager, Rails API, and managed with CocoaPods.
